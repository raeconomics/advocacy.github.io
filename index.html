    Understanding and Solving the Racial and Gender Gap in Growth Equity

    Chasing the Bag

    This platform aims to shed light on the significant disparities in the amount of funding received by minority entrereneurs, providing insights and data to foster understanding and drive change.

    About Us

    Welcome to our website, where our mission is to bridge the racial gap in private market funding and foster an inclusive entrepreneurial ecosystem. We believe that diversity is a catalyst for innovation, and we are committed to leveling the playing field for minority entrepreneurs who have historically been marginalized.

    Data

    The racial disparity in private market funding is stark. Despite representing a significant portion of the population, Black and Latinx entrepreneurs receive a disproportionately small share of venture capital and private equity investments.
    According to a 2020 report by the Harvard Business School, Black entrepreneurs received only 1% of venture capital funding, while Latinx entrepreneurs received just 1.8%. In contrast, white entrepreneurs secured 77% of the funding.
    This disparity is further reflected in the broader landscape of startup investments. The 2021 Diversity in Startups report by RateMyInvestor and Diversity VC found that only 2.4% of venture capital dollars went to Black-founded startups, and Latinx founders received 1.7% of the funding. These figures illustrate a persistent inequity that stifles innovation and economic growth.

    Resources for Black Founders and Entrepreneurs

    Black Founders

    Black Founders is dedicated to increasing the number of successful black entrepreneurs in technology. They provide networking opportunities, educational programs, and events to support black tech entrepreneurs at various stages of their careers.

    Blacks In Technology (BIT) Foundation

    Blacks In Technology (BIT) is a global platform for Black people in technology. They offer mentorship, networking, and resources such as webinars, conferences, and a community forum to support Black professionals and entrepreneurs in the tech industry.

    Backstage Capital

    Backstage Capital is a venture capital fund that invests in underrepresented founders, including Black entrepreneurs. They provide funding, resources, and support to help diverse startups succeed. Their mission is to change the face of entrepreneurship by providing opportunities for underrepresented groups.
